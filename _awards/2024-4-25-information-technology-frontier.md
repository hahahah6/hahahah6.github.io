In the 2024 National College Student Electronic Design Competition, where we developed a high-throughput phenotyping monitoring system for potted plants. Our project aimed to address the challenges of traditional plant cultivation by implementing a system capable of real-time monitoring of key growth parameters, such as plant height, soil nutrition, and overall plant health.

The hardware architecture included a Renesas RA6M5 microcontroller as the central control unit, with various sensors integrated into the system, including soil, spectral, and laser sensors. These sensors enabled the collection of real-time data, which was processed and analyzed to optimize water and fertilizer usage through dynamic control. The system also featured a cloud-based human-computer interaction interface built with Vue.js, allowing remote monitoring and control of plant conditions.
